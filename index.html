⚠ Safety First: Lumbar Disc & Rotator Cuff Protection

  • Train in a pain-free, controlled range. Stop for sharp pain, pins and needles, radiating symptoms, sudden weakness or symptoms that worsen after training.
  • Prioritize neutral spine, ribs stacked over pelvis, light core brace and slow breathing on every set.
  • For shoulder work, keep shoulders down, avoid painful overhead ranges, avoid aggressive internal rotation and use moderate loads.
  • Use machines and back support where available. Progress load only when form is consistent.

Upper Chest, Back & Core
Upper chest · upper back · posture · core

Warm Up

  • Treadmill walk — 5 min
  • Shoulder circles — slow controlled
  • Band pull aparts — light tension

Cardio

10–15 min incline walk after lifting.

Session Notes

  • Keep pressing pain-free; prioritize shoulder blades down/back.
  • For cable external rotations, use a very light load.
Exercise Order
1
Incline Chest Press Machine
4 × 10–12

Muscles: Upper chest, anterior deltoid, triceps

Focus: Upper chest and front shoulders

Start
Finish
✓ Do This
  • Set seat so handles align with upper chest
  • Keep shoulder blades gently set
  • Maintain tall chest with ribs down
  • Press up and forward while exhaling
  • Keep neck long and relaxed
✗ Avoid This
  • Excessive low-back arching
  • Dropping elbows too deep
  • Flaring elbows aggressively
  • Shrugging shoulders
  • Forcing a painful stretch
ℹ Safety Notes
  • Use a comfortable range for the rotator cuff
  • Stop before any shoulder pinch or instability
  • Keep the core engaged and lumbar spine neutral
2
Chest Press Machine
3 × 12

Muscles: Pectoralis major, anterior deltoid, triceps

Focus: Chest, triceps, shoulder-friendly pressing

Start
Finish
✓ Do This
  • Set seat so handles line up with mid-chest
  • Keep shoulders down and back
  • Brace core and maintain neutral spine
  • Press smoothly and exhale
  • Keep wrists straight and neck relaxed
✗ Avoid This
  • Flaring elbows too wide
  • Shrugging or rolling shoulders forward
  • Arching the low back
  • Jutting the head forward
  • Locking out hard or bouncing the weight
ℹ Safety Notes
  • Use a pain-free range and moderate load
  • Stop if the front of the shoulder pinches
  • Keep lumbar spine supported against the pad

Finish

Safety & Medical Notes
General education only — not a substitute for professional medical care

General education only. This handbook is not a diagnosis, treatment plan or substitute for care from a physician or physiotherapist.

A history of lumbar disc herniation and partial rotator cuff tear warrants individualized clearance, especially if symptoms are recent, worsening or neurological.

Seek medical attention promptly for progressive weakness, numbness, bowel/bladder changes, severe night pain, unexplained weight loss or pain after trauma.

🚨 Red Flags — Stop Training & Seek Advice
  • Sharp or increasing shoulder pain, catching, painful clicking, loss of strength or inability to lift the arm comfortably.
  • Pain radiating down the leg, numbness, tingling, loss of coordination or any change in bowel/bladder control.
  • Back pain that worsens during or after exercise despite reduced load and range of motion.
  • Dizziness, chest pain, shortness of breath beyond expected exertion or unusual symptoms during cardio or sauna use.

Daily Cue Reminders

  • Neutral spine at all times
  • Core engagement before every set
  • Rotator cuff protection — no painful ranges
  • Shoulder blades down and back
  • Neck neutral, not forward
  • Breathe — exhale on the effort
